Testimonials of Korean Comfort Women

In 1932, the Japanese military set up a system of “comfort stations” in countries where Japanese soldiers served abroad. Between 1932 and 1945, thousands of Korean women from poor, rural families were drafted to become “comfort women.” They were imprisoned in Japanese comfort stations and were raped and abused by Japanese soldiers. This project presents testimonials of three Korean comfort women who survived and returned to Korea in 1945. This project aims to provide insights into the experience that left Korean comfort women with severe physical and emotional scars.
